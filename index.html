Key Terms Glossary

  • LLM (Large Language Model): An AI model trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language.

  • Prompt Engineering: The art and science of crafting effective inputs (prompts) to guide a generative model to produce a desired output.

  • Diffusion Model: A type of generative model, often used for creating images, that learns to build a clear image from random noise.

  • Hallucination: An event where an AI confidently produces incorrect or nonsensical information that was not present in its training data.

  • Token: The basic unit of text (a word or part of a word) that a language model processes.

  • Fine-Tuning: The process of adapting a pre-trained model with additional, task-specific data to specialize its outputs.

  • Multimodal Model: A generative system that can understand and create content across multiple data types such as text, images, or audio.

  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG): A technique that combines external knowledge retrieval with a generative model to produce more grounded, up-to-date responses.
